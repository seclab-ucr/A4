Tour or Visit the UW Campus

As part of the Front Range of the Rocky Mountains, the University of Wyoming offers one of the most beautiful campuses in the nation. Our region’s natural beauty is matched with cutting-edge learning facilities that make for an unbeatable student experience.

  • Take a tour
  • Schedule a campus visit
  • Find an admissions counselor

University of Wyoming

  1. Marian H. Rochelle Gateway Center

    Welcome to the University of Wyoming! We are here in the Marian H. Rochelle Gateway Center, the new front door of the university. This is where you'll begin and end your UW experience, as it is home to both our Office of Admissions and career placement services. You'll also find the McMurry Foundation UW Legacy Hall, where interactive displays tell the university's fascinating history, describe our traditions, feature our notable alumni and point to the outstanding academic programs that make UW one of the great universities in the Rocky Mountain West. We hope you enjoy your visit to our beautiful and modern campus!
    Before we get started, let me explain the many ways you can explore our beautiful space.

    -You can click on the forward facing arrow to start walking around.
    -You can jump to the next stop by clicking on the "Next" button.
    -Now, if you already know where you want to go, you can select any location by using the list OR by clicking directly on the map.
    -Lastly, remember that you can explore any particular location in more detail by clicking the supplemental icons.

  2. Visual Arts Building and Centennial Complex

    The university's vibrant visual arts programs are located here in the new Visual Arts Building and the UW Centennial Complex, home of the renowned UW Art Museum as well as the American Heritage Center, one of the biggest historical archives in the West. This is where our students create and display their drawings, paintings, photographs, sculpture, ceramics and more. These buildings have received national attention for their innovative architecture and energy efficiency.

  3. Buchanan Center for the Performing Arts

    At UW, we know that artistic expression and academic success go hand in hand. That's why we have one of the top performing arts centers in the West, the Buchanan Center for the Performing Arts. This is where our music, theater and dance students hone their crafts alongside faculty members who are top performers themselves, and where people from around Wyoming come to watch an array of concerts and productions throughout the year. That's what drew me here: My classmates and I can be involved. We all know each other. We all support one another. And that lives up to one of UW's core beliefs, that the arts are an integral part of a full life.

  4. Fraternity/Sorority Row and Honors House

    The university's vibrant Greek community is centered here on Fraternity/Sorority Row, along with the Honors House, where students in UW's Honors College can choose to live. The large grassy area between the fraternities and sororities is used by students for volleyball, Frisbee, studying and many other activities. Many of our fraternity and sorority members also are members of the more than 200 student organizations across campus, and our Honors College is expanding to further enrich UW's undergraduate experience.

  5. Half Acre Recreation and Wellness Center

    An education is more than just studying – it's an invitation to explore the world. Half Acre is a state-of-the-art fitness center that helps you stay fit in mind and body. Here at UW, we have clubs and activities that not only help you stay healthy, but they also encourage you to get out of the classroom and investigate the real world. Our Campus Recreation Program will rent you the canoes, climbing gear, snowshoes and other items you need to pursue such adventure. And boy, do we have some gorgeous terrain to explore in Wyoming!

  6. Prexy’s Pasture

    Prexy's Pasture is the heart of the UW campus, giving a wonderful view of the university's iconic sandstone buildings that house the colleges of education, agriculture, engineering, and arts and sciences. Believe it or not, this originally was a buffalo wallow that became a pasture for UW's first president. Today, Prexy's Pasture serves as a great place for students to recreate, relax, study or just enjoy Wyoming's blue, sunny skies.

  7. Geological Museum

    One of the gems of the UW campus is the historic Geological Museum, home to "Big Al," the most complete Allosaurus fossil ever found, and a 75-foot Apatosaurus skeleton. Wyoming is internationally known for its dinosaur discoveries, and our geology and earth science programs are strong. Just outside the front door of the museum is a full-sized T-Rex made of copper. The legend on campus is that if you can throw a spruce or pine cone into the T-Rex's mouth during Finals Week, you'll get an A on each final exam.

  8. Energy Innovation Center

    UW strives to make education interactive. Here in the Shell 3-D Visualization Center in our Energy Innovation Center, we get the opportunity to go inside the world at a microscopic level, or to build a world and walk around in it. It turns wrought data into a dynamic, virtual-reality experience. That's what UW is all about – harnessing technology to create a more enriching experience for everyone.

  9. Michael B. Enzi STEM Facility

    At UW, there is amazing opportunity for students to be involved in research. It all starts here at the Michael B. Enzi STEM Facility, our new laboratory building where just about everyone who takes a science course participates in lab experiences. Be it chemistry, physics or biology, this is where you'll get the scientific foundation as an undergraduate to participate in research projects that are available only to graduate students at many other universities. It's another example of how UW combines the opportunities of a major research university with the advantages of a smaller school.

  10. Ben Franklin Statue

    Near our College of Arts and Sciences, our iconic statue of Benjamin Franklin serves as a reminder of the need to pursue a balanced life and education – mixing scientific discovery with the arts and humanities, along with international experiences. The Cheney International Center, named for the former vice president and UW alumnus, is the base of our exchange and study-abroad programs, which send students to over 400 sites around the world. From here, you also can see some of UW's historic buildings, including Old Main and Merica Hall.

  11. Simpson Plaza

    One of the busiest parts of campus is Simpson Plaza, at the end of Prexy's Pasture and near our student union, the library and other academic buildings. Here, there are weekly summer concerts and student activities throughout the school year. The rocks and trees provide a great place to gather and relax, and they reflect the beautiful scenery that surrounds our high-elevation community.

  12. Wyoming Union

    This is the hub of campus life and a focal point for all student activities. Here, we can do everything from grab a bite at the food court, to buy our textbooks and UW apparel, to enjoy a concert or comedy performance, study or play a little pool or ping pong. Our student government offices are here, and there's even a student art gallery to check out. You'll also find information about the many clubs and activities our students participate in.

  13. Coe Library

    If you're looking for a place to study, or to get some help in a difficult class, this is the place to go. Coe Library has silent and group study areas, a big 24-hour computer lab, even a research room with 3D printers – and, of course, all the books and publications you need, with online networks linking to extensive databases of library resources. Walk-in student tutoring takes place here, and the Learning Resource Center and Writing Center provide free academic assistance. Good students are those who ask for help when they need it, and this is the place to find it.

  14. College of Business

    Welcome to UW's College of Business, one of the Rocky Mountain region's premier business schools. The state-of the-art facility is the meeting ground for tomorrow's industry leaders. Here, students enjoy small class sizes and personalized attention from professors, as well as internship opportunities that take them out of the classroom and into the board rooms of top companies. We equip students with the tools to make immediate contributions to the organizations they join upon graduation. Because here at UW, we believe in building a better society through better business.

  15. Washakie Dining Center/Residence Halls

    Whether you live in one of the residence halls or separately buy a meal package, Washakie Dining Center is a great place to meet your nutritional needs. The wide array of food options include sandwiches made to order, a pizza bar, a deli and a Mongolian grill. While there are specified meal times, there's all-day access to pizza, soups and salads, and a breakfast bar. The residence halls, which connect to the Washakie Center by underground tunnels, are some of Wyoming's tallest buildings.

  16. Athletics Complex

    UW is a Division I member of the NCAA and the Mountain West Conference, with 17 intercollegiate men's and women's varsity sports. The Cowboys and Cowgirls are Wyoming's home teams, with a strong and passionate fan base throughout the state and across the country. War Memorial Stadium and the Arena-Auditorium are the newly renovated, major venues for football and basketball, and our Olympic sports share some of the best facilities in the Mountain West – including the one-of-a-kind High Altitude Performance Center. Our students attend athletics events free of charge, and they give the Cowboys and Cowgirls a strong home-field and home-court advantage!
