Let us show you around

Virtual Tour. Campus is only a click away.

Biola University

  1. Metzger Lawn

    Welcome to Biola University! My name is Brittany and I'll be your tour guide today. I'm excited to show you around our Southern California campus and give you a look at what makes Biola one of the nation's leading Christian universities. Here at Biola, it's our mission to provide biblically centered education, scholarship and service equipping men and women in mind and character to impact the world for the Lord Jesus Christ. Throughout the tour, you'll learn more about how this mission plays out in students' day-to-day lives


    But before we get started, let me explain how you can make the most of this virtual campus tour

    - You can click on the front facing arrow to start walking around our campus

    - You can also jump to the next stop by clicking on the next stop button

    - Now, if you already know where you want to go, you can select any location by using the list on the left OR by clicking directly on the map

    - Lastly, remember that you can explore any particular location in more detail by clicking on the 360, Photos, or Video icons

  2. Thank You For Visiting

    I hope you've enjoyed your tour of Biola! While you've seen a lot today, you haven't seen everything. I encourage you to visit in person if you can and get a feel for our campus' excitement and passion for impacting the world for Christ. Thank you for joining us, and may God bless you in your college search process

  3. Crowell School of Business

    Here we are at the Business Building, home to Biola's Crowell School of Business. Business Administration is our most popular major, but with over 40 majors and 80 concentrations to choose from, we have a solid academic presence across the board. In fact, Biola is ranked as a first-tier national university by US News & World Report. It also has a 16-to-1 student to faculty ratio, with 75 percent of classes having fewer than 25 students. Something unique about Biola's academics is that all regular students take 30 credits of Bible and theology classes. As you grow in the knowledge of your major, you'll also grow in your knowledge of the Bible, too.

  4. Production Center

    The Production Center is considered home by many of our Journalism, Public Relations, and Cinema and Media Arts students. This 10,000-square-foot facility is open 24 hours a day and contains sound stages, a control room, a screening room, editing bays, the Convergent Newsroom, the Equipment Room and more.

    At the Equipment Room, students can rent thousands of dollars worth of professional equipment to use for school and personal projects. The Convergent Newsroom allows students to access professional software such as FinalCut Pro and Adobe Creative Suite.

    All these resources allow Biola to have some of the country's top programs in journalism, public relations, and film. In fact, Biola's School of Cinema and Media Arts was recently named one of the world's top 50 film schools by Variety, a leading industry publication.

  5. The Lim Center

    Welcome to the Alton and Lydia Lim Center for Science, Technology and Health the newest and largest classroom building on campus. This innovative facility serves as a home for the next generation of scientists, researchers, health professionals, engineers and technology developers. It houses 30 laboratories, three classrooms, research spaces, a rooftop observatory and all-new, cutting-edge technology. All that to say, it's an exciting time to be a science student at Biola!

  6. Library

    Biola's Library is one of students' favorite spots to get down to business. It's a perfect place to study indoors, and it's stocked with more than just books. Inside, you'll also find Mac and PC computer labs, group study rooms, media equipment and movies you can check out for free, and more. The Learning Center and Writing Center are located here as well, offering valuable services at low or no cost to students. The Library's newest addition, Heritage Cafe, gives students delicious options for fueling up for the day's activities and plenty of space to enjoy socializing in the sunshine.

  7. Calvary Chapel & Talbot East

    As you already know by now, it's Biola's intention to grow students both academically and spiritually. In addition to taking 30 credits of Bible and theology classes, students also attend 20 chapels each semester. Calvary Chapel is just one of the many locations where chapel services are held every week. This building was renovated in 2018 by world-renowned Danish artists Peter Brandes and Maja Lisa Engelhardt, who transformed the space with original sculptures and vibrant stained-glass windows.

    Next to Calvary Chapel is one of our newest buildings, Talbot East. This building is home to our undergraduate biblical and theological studies program. Talbot East has some of my favorite features about Biola's campus, including the Scripture windows, Riady Rooftop Garden, Haqq Plaza courtyard and reflection pool, and the Fred and Ruth Waugh Prayer Chapel.

  8. Mosaic Cultural Center

    At Biola, we're passionate about diversity. Behind me is the Mosaic Cultural Center, which offers opportunities for students from different backgrounds to engage with and learn from each other. Housed in this space is the Student Enrichment and Intercultural Development office, known as SEID. They put on events such as SCORR, the Student Congress on Racial Reconciliation. This national conference brings together students, staff and faculty from various Christian institutions to celebrate and explore diversity within the body of Christ

  9. Bell Tower & Art Gallery

    Here is the famous Biola Bell Tower, a campus fixture since 1987. These bells were first placed at Biola's original location in downtown Los Angeles in 1915 and used to ring out hymns through the city streets. Today, students can still hear the bells chime multiple times each day.

    Speaking of celebrating the past, have you heard of the Torrey Honors Institute? This is Biola's classical education program, based on the Socratic method or Oxford style of learning. Instead of taking general education and Bible classes, Torrey students earn their credits by reading and discussing the great books that have shaped history.

  10. Fluor Fountain

    Welcome to Fluor Fountain, a Biola landmark that's right at the center of campus. Because we're located in Southern California, you'll find students studying, socializing and sunning year-round here. Campus groups and visitors alike often set up information tables closeby to keep students in-the-know about extracurricular and ministry opportunities.

    On the side of Bardwell Hall is Biola's most recognizable piece of art: "The Word." This mural was donated by renowned L.A. artist Kent Twitchell in 1989. Although it's commonly called "the Jesus Mural," it actually portrays the full Trinity. In the painting, Jesus casts two shadows which represent the Father and the Holy Spirit. Let's move on now to learn more about student life at Biola.

  11. Student Union Building

    This is our Student Union Building, which is home to our Student Government Association, Student Programming and Activities, our campus newspaper (The Chimes), the department of Spiritual Life, the Student Missionary Union and more.

    Biola's Student Missionary Union is the largest student-led missions organization in the U.S. The group commissions dozens of students to participate in short-term mission trips during each school break, and also puts on the largest annual student-run Missions Conference in the world. Biola's Student Programming and Activities, or SPA, oversees clubs and provides students with events and activities such as Punk 'n Pie, Nationball, Outdoor Excursions, Mock Rock, intramural sports and more

  12. Biola Caf

    Biola's Cafeteria, more commonly known as The Caf, is a staple of most undergraduates' experience. Meal plans for on-campus students range in size from 10 to 20 meals per week. The Caf has six stations inside, all catered by the award-winning Bon Apptit food service. In addition to The Caf, students can choose to dine at other on-campus restaurants and coffee shops: Heritage Cafe, Blackstone Cafe, Eagles' Nest, Common Grounds and The Talon.

  13. Conservatory of Music

    Here we are at Crowell Hall, the building that houses Biola's Conservatory of Music. There are often music performances here from students and visiting renowned artists. The building has a main auditorium as well as multiple practice rooms, which are open to music students 24/7.

    Across the way is our Fireplace Pavilion, which offers shade during the day and a fireplace with additional heaters to keep you toasty at night. Next to the pavilion are the Mailboxes, where students can go to receive their letters and packages, or send some of their own.

  14. Chase Gymnasium

    Welcome to Chase Gym, home of the Biola Eagles' athletic programs. Biola is a member of the NCAA Division II and competes against other universities across the region as part of the PacWest Conference.

    For those who don't plan to play on a sports team in college, but still want to be active, Biola has intramural sports, an outdoor track and soccer field, a pool and a fitness center available for student use.

  15. Horton Hall

    Here we are at Horton Hall. This is just one of Biola's eight dorms. Inside, Horton has a main atrium-style lobby, a lounge on every floor, community bathrooms, laundry facilities and more

    When it comes to Biola's dorms, the features vary, but one thing that's consistent is the community that's formed here. All the Resident Advisers, or RAs, work to plan awesome events and foster deep relationships among the students on their floors.

  16. Blackstone Hall

    Our newest dorm is Blackstone Hall, which is filled with amenities to enhance community-building. There are co-ed lounges, an outdoor courtyard on the top floor, whiteboard walls that students can write on, and did I mention Blackstone has its very own dining facility? Blackstone Cafe serves breakfast all day, and it's a convenient place for students to grab a meal, drink or snack without straying too far from their dorm.
