Berkeley Protocol

Berkeley Protocol gives guidance on using public digital info to fight for human rights

The first international protocol on using social media as evidence of human rights violations, launches today. The Berkeley Protocol a joint publication of the UN Human Rights Office and UC Berkeleys Human Rights Centre -- offers global guidance on using public digital information.

Slave trade The High Commissioner addressed the European Parliament at the Inaugural Commemoration of the European Day for the Abolition of the Slave Trade. She said racial discrimination is a pervasive human rights violation and that recognizing the past is the first step to repairing the present and key to building the future.
Racism and slavery Speaking on the international day for the abolition of slavery, the High Commissioner noted that the pandemic increases the risk that people will be enslaved and called for concerted global action
Berkeley Protocol The High Commissioner sent a video message to the launch of the Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations. the first global guideline for using public digital information as evidence in international criminal and human rights investigations.
Human Rights in Latin America The High Commissioner spoke about human rights and women's leadership in Latin America in the context of COVID-19 in an event organized by Georgetown University. She emphasized that human rightd are the way out of the current crisis and "into the world we want"
Climate and children Today's children and future generations have a right to thrive in a greener world, the High Commissioner pointed out. And global recognition of the right to a safe, clean, healthy and sustainable environment could drive stronger policies to protect them
Minorities and COVID In an important speech, the High Commissioner pointed out that due to discrimination, racial minorities and indigenous peoples communities have been over-exposed, under-protected and disproportionately harmed
Amazon defender The High Commissioner hailed the award of the Edelstam Prize to Brazilian human rights defender Osvalinda Marcelino Alves Pereira
Women and conflict COVID-19 may increase the already high risks of gender-based violence in conflict settings including sexual violence, child marriage and sexual exploitation. It also prevents many victims from accessing justice and protection
