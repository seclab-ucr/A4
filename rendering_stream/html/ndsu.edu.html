North Dakota State University

  1. Bison Statue Welcome

    Welcome to North Dakota State University! We are an affordable mid-sized research university with a focus on students located in Fargo, ND. We can't wait to show you all that you will experience as a student at NDSU.

    Before we get started, let me explain the many ways you can explore our beautiful space.

    -You can click on the forward facing arrow to start walking around.
    -You can jump to the next stop by clicking on the "Next" button.
    -Now, if you already know where you want to go, you can select any location by using the list OR by clicking directly on the map.
    -Lastly, remember that you can explore any particular location in more detail by clicking the supplemental icons.

  2. Memorial Union - Main Level

    The Memorial Union is the heart of campus and is the hub of student activity. It houses offices for Student Activities, Student Government, Multicultural Programs, International Student and Study Abroad, and LGBTQ+ resources. If you're looking for a way to get involved outside of the classroom, the Union is a great place to start. Grab your friends for a free movie in our on campus theater, visit the art gallery, or explore our 300 student clubs and organizations that frequently hold events and meetings in the Union. The main floor also houses our campus bookstore where you can grab supplies for class or your favorite apparel for Bison Pride Friday.

  3. Memorial Union - Lower Level

    As you make your way downstairs, you will find a great place to hang out with friends. The Rec center includes a seven lane bowling alley, esports gaming lab, billiards, and more. If you're hungry visit the Union Dining Center, included in your unlimited meal plan. You can also check out the food court that offers everything from asian and mexican to burgers, salads, subs, and pizza. Grab a seat with a friend and listen to Live at Lunch for some midday entertainment or come back in the evening for fun, free events hosted by campus attractions.

  4. Ceres Hall

    Ceres Hall is full of resources to help you succeed including the Office of Admission, the Career and Advising Center, and the Counseling Center. The Career & Advising center is here to help you explore majors, apply for internships, and prepare for your future career. At the counseling center, you will find knowledgable staff (and calming therapy dogs) who provide individual and group support throughout your college experience.

  5. A. Glenn Hill Classrooms

    Interactive learning is a cornerstone of the NDSU experience. A Glenn Hill is the largest classroom building on campus. With a focus on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics classes, all students have the opportunity to learn in state-of-the-art interactive learning spaces and unique private study rooms. The second and third floors of the building are popular destinations for their comfy study spaces surrounded by windows and natural light. This is where you will also find our impressive biology and chemistry labs. Most of your classes will be forty students or less resulting in exciting conversation, innovative learning, and the opportunity to get to know your professors.

  6. Wallman Wellness Center

    Staying healthy is easy at the Wallman Wellness Center. The Wellness Center provides you with state-of-the-art fitness equipment, recreation and intramural sports programs and club sports. It also offers a high-quality and affordable child care center for students. Our facility includes a 34-foot climbing wall, strength and cardiovascular equipment, racquetball and basketball courts, group fitness studios, multi-activity court, and walking and running tracks. It also houses an impressive aquatic addition offering a lap pool, leisure pool, fire pit, sauna, and more. If you're feeling under the weather, here you will also find the on-campus clinic and pharmacy, Student Health Services, for access to quality health care right on campus.

  7. Cater Residence Hall

    Cater Hall is one of our many on-campus housing options that provide convience, safety, community, and variety to meet your housing needs. All students live on campus their first year at NDSU and choose between traditional or suite style residence hall. Here in Cater, you will find suite style rooms, rec lounges, study spaces, a community kitchen, wifi, coin-less laundry and so much more. Upperclassmen and graduate students have the option to live anywhere on campus including our on-campus apartments. Living on campus at NDSU is truly your home away from home.

  8. West Dining Center

    At NDSU, our Dining Centers provide an array of food options, including homestyle classics, global cuisine, fresh salad and sandwich bars, and grills for you to create your own, unique meal. For students with allergies, NDSU Dining is at the forefront of allergen-friendly service. The West Dining Center has a food station free of the top eight allergens and is a peanut- and tree nut-free facility. In addition to our staples, the NDSU Dining Centers also has delicious specials, including Thanksgiving Dinner and Late Night Breakfast during Finals Week. Our dining centers are the perfect place to meet up with friends and enjoy the beloved oreo cookie salad.

  9. Aldevron Tower

    Aldevron Tower is home to more than 2,000 undergraduate, professional, and graduate students in the College of Health Professions. Here students practice hands-on skills and utilize technology to enhance their learning. Facilities feature the latest advances in the field including an interactive concept pharmacy and nursing simulation and skills labs. Students in the College of Health Professions benefit from internships or clinical rotations that enhance learning and provide the skills necessary to excel in their field.

  10. Engineering Complex

    We are now standing outside of our Engineering Complex, home to state-of-the-art lab spaces, and countless research opportunities. The College of Engineering offers 12 undergraduate degrees and more than 26 concentrations so you can find a program that's a perfect fit for you.

  11. Babbling Brook

    The Babbling Brook is one of the beautiful outdoor spaces on campus, located between Minard Hall and South Engineering. In the outdoor atrium, students sit and study, enjoy the outdoors, and take in the beauty of campus. Programs like Construction Management, Horticulture, Entomology, and Photography will often be seen around this spot, taking the classroom outdoors.

  12. Minard Hall

    You will have access to so much on campus, from IT support and computers in every building to faculty who have scheduled hours every week devoted to answering your questions and serving you. When you're at student at NDSU, it's very likely that you will visit Minard Hall, as many academic departments, faculty offices, and classrooms are housed within this building. Between classes, you can also grab a coffee or snack at the Minard Coffee Shop.

  13. Reineke Fine Arts Center

    Home to our Division of Performing Arts, the Reinecke Fine Arts Center hosts theatre productions, festivals, concerts, and community arts events every week of the year. Student tickets are free for any NDSU event, and heavily discounted for the Fargo-Moorhead Symphony Orchestra, Fargo-Moorhead Opera, and Fargo-Moorhead Ballet. Students can develop their creative potential by participating in Theatre NDSU or through any of our many vocal or instrumental music ensembles. Take a class, take lessons, or audition to be a part of the many artistic opportunities at NDSU.

  14. Putnam Hall and Old Main

    Putnam Hall is one of the oldest buildings on campus and once housed a Carnegie Library. Now, Putnam Hall is home to NDSU's Graduate School. We offer a wide range of doctoral degrees, master's degrees, and graduate certificate programs. Our graduate students represent nearly every state and 80 countries. Career outcomes for our graduate students are excellent, and most of our graduate students recieve funding in the form of graduate assistantships, grants, or employer reimbursement.

  15. Library

    The library is the most popular study location on campus. Our helpful librarians can assist you with research projects and help you find resources for class. Here you will also find helpful resources like our math emporium, center for writers, and disability services. Reserve a study room for your group project or nestle in for a study session on the nearly silent third floor. During finals week, the library is open 24 hours a day and provides destressing activities like coloring pages, therapy dog cuddles, and support to help you end your semester strong.

  16. Sanford Health Athletic Complex (SHAC)

    NDSU features 16 Division I varsity sports and is a member of The Summit League and Missouri Valley Football Conference with an affiliate membership in the Big 12 Conference for wrestling. NDSU has a storied history of athletic success including 28 team national championships since 1965 in football, women's basketball, wrestling, softball, men's cross country and women's indoor track and field.

  17. Renaissance Hall

    The beautiful Renaissance Hall located in a historical and rennovated building in downtown fargo houses our departments of visual arts, architecture, and landscape architecture. This building includes studios and classrooms for graphic design, photography, printmaking, ceramics, painting, and drawing. Students also have access to a woodshop, computer labs, gallery, and an outdoor sculpture garden. Transportation to our downtown campuses is free and easy through the MAT bus or bike share program. The buses run every few minutes from main campus and the surrounding areas making getting to class a breeze.

  18. Richard H. Barry Hall

    Our College of Business is located in Downtown Fargo at Richard H. Barry Hall, giving our students direct access to area businesses, entrepreneurial opportunities, and professional development events. Additionally, the college works hard to directly connect students to local businessmen and women through our Mentorship Program. This allows students to grow their network before they enter the job market, exploring their options and building connections within the community. NDSU business students also have awesome resources available to them, including the Commodity Trading Room, live stock market feed, and corporate board room spaces.
